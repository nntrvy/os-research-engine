# Vietnam's Top Problems & Pain Points: Market Research Report

**Research Date:** January 24, 2026
**Data Period:** 2024-2025
**Researcher:** OSR-Researcher

---

## Executive Summary

This report identifies and analyzes the top problems and pain points facing Vietnamese consumers and businesses across five critical domains: Economic, Education, Healthcare, Urban Living, and Financial Services. Key findings include:

- **Urban Air Pollution Crisis:** Hanoi ranked world's most polluted city (Jan 2025), costing $14B annually (7% of GDP)
- **Education Skills Gap:** 95% of graduates lack workforce-ready skills; 77-point digital divide between rich/poor children
- **Healthcare Overload:** Hospital occupation rates hit 200% in central facilities; $27.5B total health expenditure (2025)
- **SME Financing Gap:** $24 billion unmet financing needs (2.11x current lending levels); only 9.3% of SMEs access bank loans
- **Insurance Penetration Gap:** 1.3% of GDP vs. 3.5-8.7% in regional peers; life insurance contracting for 3rd consecutive year

**Market Context:**
- Population: 101.6 million (2025)
- Middle class: 26% of population (2026 projection), expanding to 55% by 2030
- Average monthly income: VND 5.4 million (~$213 USD)
- Consumer spending: $264.7 billion (2025)

## 1. Economic Problems

### 1.1 Cost of Living & Inflation

**Current Status (2025):**
- CPI increased 3.31% year-on-year (2025), meeting National Assembly target
- H1 2025 average CPI: +3.27% YoY; core inflation +3.16%
- Average worker income: VND 8.3 million/month (+10.1% YoY)

**Key Price Drivers:**
- **Food & Services:** +3.78% (contributing 1/3 of CPI rise)
  - Pork prices: +12% due to supply shortages and holiday demand
- **Housing & Utilities:** +5.11%
  - Construction materials (cement, steel) driving increases
- **Exchange Rate Pressure:** Currency depreciation contributing to imported inflation

**Pain Points Identified:**
1. Food price volatility impacts household budgets disproportionately
2. Housing costs rising faster than overall inflation
3. Construction material costs affecting home ownership affordability

**Market Size:**
- Total consumer spending: $264.7 billion (2025)
- Household disposable income per capita: $3,320 (2025)
- Real earnings growth: +4.8% annually through 2029 (projected)

### 1.2 Income Inequality

**Current Status:**
- Gini coefficient: Lower than Malaysia/Philippines; similar to Indonesia, Lao PDR, Thailand
- Extreme poverty: <1% (2022), down from 45% (1992)
- Middle class emerging: 26% of population (2026), projected 55% by 2030

**Pain Points:**
1. **Informal Employment:** 2/3 of workers in informal jobs lacking social protection
2. **Urban-Rural Divide:** Middle class concentrated in Hanoi/HCMC
3. **Export Dependency Risk:** Economic growth facing headwinds from trade barriers

**Underserved Segments:**
- 67% of workforce in informal employment without social security
- Rural populations with limited access to economic opportunities
- Workers vulnerable to global trade disruptions

**Data Source Date:** OECD Economic Surveys: Viet Nam 2025 (published 2025)

## 2. Education Problems

### 2.1 Skills Gap & Graduate Readiness

**Critical Problems:**
- Employers cite **persistent gaps in graduate readiness**
- Skill shortages in high-demand sectors:
  - Semiconductors
  - Renewable energy
  - ICT
  - Logistics
  - Healthcare

**Curriculum Inadequacy:**
- Lower and upper secondary education **fails to prepare students for workforce**
- Students lack necessary knowledge and skills for future careers
- Industry-academia mismatch in competencies

**Market Size:**
- Government education expenditure: $18.7 billion (14.4% of national budget)
- EdTech market revenue: $364.7 million (2024)
- Projected CAGR: 11.46% (2024-2029)

### 2.2 Digital Divide & Access Disparities

**Geographic Inequality:**
- **95% of richest children** have home internet
- **Only 18% of poorest children** have home internet
- **Widest digital divide in Southeast Asia**

**Urban-Rural Gap:**
- 85% of urban households have internet (2021)
- 65% of rural households have internet (2021)
- EdTech solutions concentrated in Hanoi/HCMC; rural areas underserved

**Infrastructure Limitations:**
- Many public schools lack necessary EdTech infrastructure:
  - Outdated hardware
  - Inadequate tech facilities
  - ~3 students per computer

### 2.3 EdTech Adoption Barriers

**Policy Implementation Gaps:**
- Government policies exist but lack:
  - Specific action plans
  - Adequate funding
  - Implementation measures
- Bureaucratic constraints slow decision-making
- Missed opportunities to integrate advanced technologies

**Pain Points:**
1. Public education system too rigid
2. Teacher shortages, particularly in rural areas
3. Inadequate teacher training for digital tools
4. Limited integration of EdTech in mainstream curricula

**Underserved Segments:**
- Rural and suburban students (outside Hanoi/HCMC)
- Poorest 82% of children without home internet
- Public schools lacking modern infrastructure
- Adult learners seeking reskilling/upskilling

**Existing Solutions & Gaps:**
- Private EdTech companies focused on urban middle class
- Government digital education initiatives lack funding/execution
- Corporate training programs insufficient for skills demand
- No scalable solutions for rural digital divide

**Data Source Date:** EdTech Hub Vietnam Rapid Scan (November 2024), UNESCO/SEAMEO reports (2024-2025)

## 3. Healthcare Problems

### 3.1 Insurance Coverage Gaps

**Current Status:**
- Health insurance coverage: 89.3% (2021), targeting 95% by 2025, 100% by 2030
- Despite high coverage, significant challenges remain:
  - **Underutilization of SHI benefits in rural areas**
  - **High out-of-pocket costs for uncovered services**
  - Overcrowding in public hospitals

**Pain Points:**
1. Insurance coverage doesn't guarantee access to quality care
2. Many services not covered by social health insurance (SHI)
3. Rural populations unable to use SHI benefits effectively
4. Fee-for-service payment methods drive cost escalation

### 3.2 Healthcare Costs

**Market Size:**
- Average healthcare spending: **$270 per person annually** (2025)
- Total health expenditure: **$27.5 billion** (2025), up from $17.4B (2019)
- Drug spending: **$6.9 billion** (2025), up from $4.7B (2019)
  - Pharmaceuticals account for **>30% of total insurance spending**

**Cost Control Failures:**
- Fee-for-service payment incentivizes overtreatment
- Increased autonomy of public hospitals drives costs
- Demand for high-quality services not covered by SHI
- **Insurance funds projected to be in deficit soon**

**Pain Points:**
1. Healthcare costs rising faster than incomes
2. Drug costs consuming disproportionate share of health spending
3. Out-of-pocket expenses for uncovered premium services
4. Insurance fund sustainability crisis looming

### 3.3 Access & Quality Disparities

**Urban-Rural Gap:**
- High-quality facilities concentrated in **Hanoi and HCMC**
- Significant disparities in:
  - Staffing levels
  - Quality of care
  - Medical equipment availability
- Patients bypass primary facilities to visit central/provincial hospitals

**System Overload:**
- Hospital occupation rates: **200% in centrally located hospitals**
- Insufficient medical equipment and staff
- Public hospitals overcrowded vs. underutilized rural facilities

**Technology Gap:**
- **Lack of digitalization** in healthcare system
- Heavy reliance on paper-based and manual reports
- Particularly acute in public sector

**Underserved Segments:**
- Rural populations (63% of country)
- Low-income patients (accessibility/affordability gaps)
- Patients requiring specialized care (forced to travel to major cities)
- Nearly 40% of population lacking formal financial services access

**Existing Solutions & Gaps:**
- Social Health Insurance (SHI) achieves high coverage but:
  - Limited benefit packages
  - Quality concerns drive patients to private/premium options
- Private Health Insurance (PHI) growing but:
  - Only accessible to middle/upper class
  - Concentrated in urban areas
- Telemedicine emerging but:
  - Limited adoption
  - Regulatory barriers
  - Digital literacy challenges

**Market Opportunity:**
- Insurance penetration gap: 1.3% of GDP (current) vs. 3.5-8.7% (regional peers)
- 63% rural population underserved
- 40% population without formal financial services
- Microinsurance potential for low-income segments

**Data Source Date:** The Lancet Regional Health (2023), VnExpress International (2024-2025), health insurance industry reports (2025)

## 4. Urban Living Problems

### 4.1 Air Pollution Crisis

**Severity (2025):**
- **Hanoi ranked world's most polluted city** (January 2025, AirVisual)
  - Surpassed Delhi and Lahore
  - Air quality readings exceeded 300 on multiple days
  - PM2.5 levels **>6x WHO recommended limit**
- **Ho Chi Minh City pollution: 11x WHO guidelines** (early 2025)

**Health & Economic Impact:**
- **70,000 Vietnamese deaths annually** (2nd deadliest killer after cancer)
- Economic toll: **$14 billion annually (~7% of GDP)**
- Urban income reduced by **up to 20%** due to pollution

**Sources:**
- 38% of PM2.5 in Hanoi from road dust and vehicle emissions
- 30% from nearby industrial sources
- Construction activities
- Agricultural burning in surrounding areas

### 4.2 Transportation Challenges

**Vehicle Congestion:**
- **Ho Chi Minh City: >12 million private cars and motorbikes**
- Road dust and emissions major pollution contributors
- Traffic congestion reduces quality of life and productivity

**Infrastructure Delays:**
- HCMC's first metro line opened late 2024
- Additional lines may not complete before 2030
- Hanoi breaking ground on two key metro lines (2025)
- Public transport insufficient for population growth

**Pain Points:**
1. Excessive reliance on private motorbikes/cars
2. Inadequate public transportation infrastructure
3. Long commute times in major cities
4. Air quality deterioration from vehicle emissions

### 4.3 Housing Pressures

**Urbanization Strain:**
- Massive immigration to Hanoi and HCMC
- Intensified pressures on:
  - Jobs availability
  - Housing affordability
  - Social security systems
  - Basic infrastructure

**Infrastructure Gaps:**
- Rapid urban growth outpacing infrastructure development
- Housing and transportation sectors under severe pressure
- Social services unable to keep pace with population influx

### 4.4 Government Response & Opportunities

**Regulatory Actions:**
- From 2027: All cars in Hanoi/HCMC must meet **level 4 emission standards**
- Hanoi low-emission zones starting **2026**
- National Roadmap for Electric Mobility Transition:
  - Target: **50% of buses electric by 2030**
  - Target: **100% of taxis electric by 2030**

**Market Opportunities:**
- Electric vehicle adoption
- Air quality monitoring/purification solutions
- Green building materials and construction
- Sustainable urban planning services
- Public transportation technology

**Underserved Segments:**
- Urban residents suffering from pollution (millions in Hanoi/HCMC)
- Commuters lacking efficient public transport
- Lower-income urban residents priced out of housing
- Migrants from rural areas seeking urban opportunities

**Existing Solutions & Gaps:**
- Private vehicles dominate (gaps in public transport)
- Metro projects delayed by decades
- Air purifiers available but expensive
- No comprehensive urban mobility solutions
- Limited affordable housing development

**Data Source Date:** Asia Property Awards (2025), FULCRUM (2025), VnExpress International (2024-2025), Vietnam News (2025)

## 5. Financial Services Problems

### 5.1 Banking Sector Challenges

**Non-Performing Loans Crisis:**
- NPLs at **highest level in a decade**
- Driven by:
  - Real estate turbulence
  - 5th-largest commercial bank failure (2022)
- NPL recognition surged after Circular 02 expired
- Falling reserve coverage ratios signal increasing credit risk exposure

**Liquidity Pressures:**
- Deposit growth lagging behind credit expansion
- Banks diversifying to market-based funding sources
- Funding costs increasing
- Margin pressures intensifying

**Profitability Challenges:**
- NIM compression persisted into Q1 2025
- Banks relying on non-interest income (digital services, trading gains)
- Must adapt business models amid structural changes

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Tighter exposure limits
- Stricter consumer lending assessments
- Restrictions on insurance bundling
- Rapid regulatory change requires significant compliance investment

### 5.2 Credit Access Gap

**SME Financing Crisis:**
- **Total financing gap: $24 billion**
  - SME gap: $21.7 billion
  - Micro-enterprise gap: $1.9 billion
- Gap equals **2.11x current SME lending levels**
- **Equivalent to ~22% of GDP** (World Bank estimate)

**Access Disparities:**
- Only **9.3% of SMEs** access bank loans
- **Over 56% of large corporates** access bank loans
- **62% of total SME financing needs unmet**

**Real Estate Sector Vulnerabilities:**
- Continued reliance on real estate lending
- Structural vulnerabilities remain despite Q1 2025 growth

**SME Context:**
- SMEs represent **95% of total enterprises**
- Contribute **70% of GDP**
- Provide **80% of employment**
- Generate only **<10% of import-export turnover**
- Account for **<20% of total enterprise revenue**

### 5.3 Financial Inclusion Gaps

**Underserved Segments:**
- SMEs and MSMEs (financing gap)
- Rural customers (limited branch access)
- Low-income populations (40% without formal financial services)
- Credit card users (only **3% penetration**)

**Digital Trust Challenges:**
- Growing fraud and privacy threats
- Need for secure eKYC systems
- Anti-fraud technology investment required
- Data governance concerns
- Consumer education on digital financial services

**Pain Points:**
1. Collateral requirements exclude most SMEs
2. Complex loan application processes
3. High rejection rates for small businesses
4. Limited financial products for low-income segments
5. Cybersecurity concerns limiting digital adoption

### 5.4 Insurance Market Gaps

**Market Size (2025):**
- Total premium revenue: **$9.1 billion (VND 237.2 trillion)**
  - Life insurance: $6.0 billion (VND 146.1 trillion)
  - Non-life insurance: $3.4 billion (VND 88.4 trillion)

**Penetration Crisis:**
- Life insurance penetration: **1.3% of GDP**
- Regional benchmarks:
  - Thailand: 3.5%
  - Taiwan: 8.7%
- Life insurance **contracting for 3rd consecutive year**:
  - 2023: -12%
  - 2024: -5.7%
  - 2025: -1.3% (projected)

**Causes of Contraction:**
- Bancassurance sales irregularities
- Economic challenges reducing disposable income
- Consumer distrust from mis-selling scandals
- Regulatory crackdowns on aggressive sales tactics

**National Targets (2030):**
- 18% of population to hold life policies
- Overall insurance penetration: 3.5% of GDP

**Gaps & Opportunities:**
- **Rural regions:** 63% of population, very low penetration
- **Microinsurance:** 40% population without formal financial services
- **Health insurance:** Growing awareness post-COVID
  - Critical illness coverage
  - Telemedicine integration
  - Wellness programs
- **Embedded insurance:** Distribution through non-insurance platforms

**Underserved Segments:**
- Rural populations (63% of country)
- Informal workers (67% of workforce)
- SMEs lacking business insurance
- Low-income households needing microinsurance
- Young professionals avoiding traditional insurance products

**Existing Solutions & Gaps:**
- Traditional agency model declining
- Bancassurance facing regulatory scrutiny
- Digital insurance platforms emerging but limited
- No scalable microinsurance solutions
- Limited embedded insurance integration

**Data Source Date:** FiinGroup (2025), McKinsey reports (2024-2025), Lexology Banking & Finance Guide (2025), Vietnam Life Insurance Market reports (2025)

## Customer Segments - Priority Ranking

### Tier 1: Highest Priority (Large + Underserved)

1. **SMEs & MSMEs**
   - Size: 95% of enterprises, 70% of GDP, 80% of employment
   - Pain: $24B financing gap, only 9.3% access bank loans
   - Willingness to pay: High (financing is existential need)

2. **Rural Populations**
   - Size: 63% of population (~64 million people)
   - Pain: Limited healthcare access, 65% internet penetration, low insurance coverage
   - Willingness to pay: Moderate (price-sensitive but underserved)

3. **Informal Workers**
   - Size: 67% of workforce (~40+ million people)
   - Pain: No social protection, financial exclusion
   - Willingness to pay: Low to moderate (income constraints)

### Tier 2: High Growth Potential

4. **Emerging Middle Class**
   - Size: 26% population (2026), growing to 55% by 2030
   - Pain: Education, healthcare, housing affordability
   - Willingness to pay: High and increasing with income growth

5. **Urban Commuters**
   - Size: Millions in Hanoi/HCMC (12M+ vehicles in HCMC alone)
   - Pain: Pollution exposure, long commutes, traffic congestion
   - Willingness to pay: Moderate to high (quality of life impact)

6. **Students & Parents**
   - Size: Millions of school-age children
   - Pain: Skills gap, digital divide (82% poorest without internet)
   - Willingness to pay: High (education is top family priority)

### Tier 3: Niche but High-Value

7. **Young Professionals**
   - Size: Growing segment in urban areas
   - Pain: Insurance avoidance, career advancement needs
   - Willingness to pay: High (digital-first, premium for convenience)

8. **Healthcare Seekers in Rural Areas**
   - Size: Subset of 63% rural population
   - Pain: Must travel to cities for quality care
   - Willingness to pay: Moderate (barrier is access, not willingness)

## Key Assumptions Requiring Validation

Based on this secondary research, the following hypotheses require primary research validation:

### Desirability Hypotheses (Test First)

1. **We believe that** SMEs with $24B unmet financing needs will adopt alternative lending platforms if collateral requirements are reduced by 50% and approval times shortened to <48 hours.

2. **We believe that** rural households (63% of population with 65% internet penetration) will pay VND 50,000-100,000/month for offline-first EdTech solutions that work without consistent internet.

3. **We believe that** urban commuters in Hanoi/HCMC will switch to electric mobility options if total cost of ownership is within 10% of traditional motorbikes over 3 years.

4. **We believe that** 40% of the population without formal financial services will adopt microinsurance products priced at <VND 20,000/month with simplified claims processes.

5. **We believe that** informal workers (67% of workforce) will pay for portable social protection benefits they can carry across jobs and provinces.

### Feasibility Hypotheses (Test Second)

6. **We believe that** we can build a profitable SME lending platform with default rates <5% using alternative credit scoring (transaction data, e-commerce sales, utility payments) instead of traditional collateral.

7. **We believe that** we can deliver quality telemedicine services to rural areas (63% population) with acceptable diagnostic accuracy (>85%) using basic smartphone cameras and 3G connectivity.

8. **We believe that** we can create an EdTech platform that works offline and syncs with <1MB daily data usage to serve the 18% of poorest children with home internet.

### Viability Hypotheses (Test Third)

9. **We believe that** a microinsurance product for informal workers can achieve unit economics of <VND 15,000 customer acquisition cost with >60% annual retention to reach profitability within 24 months.

10. **We believe that** an urban air quality monitoring + smart commuting app can monetize through B2B2C partnerships (insurance discounts, government contracts) and reach 100,000 users within 12 months in Hanoi/HCMC.

## Data Limitations & Gaps

### Recency Concerns
- Some insurance data from 2021-2023 (approaching 12-month limit)
- Urbanization statistics may not reflect latest 2025 migration patterns
- SME financing gap estimates based on pre-2025 data

### Missing Data
- Granular breakdown of insurance penetration by income segment
- Specific cost comparisons: public vs. private healthcare
- SME default rates by sector and size
- Rural vs. urban willingness-to-pay for digital services
- Actual EdTech adoption rates in public schools (beyond infrastructure stats)

### Data Quality Issues
- Life insurance market showing conflicting growth projections
- NPL figures may underreport due to forbearance policies
- Informal economy data inherently difficult to measure accurately

### Primary Research Required
- Customer interviews with SME owners on financing alternatives
- Ethnographic research in rural areas on healthcare/education workarounds
- Willingness-to-pay surveys for insurance/financial products
- User testing of digital-first solutions with low-income segments
- Deep-dive case studies of successful vs. failed EdTech implementations

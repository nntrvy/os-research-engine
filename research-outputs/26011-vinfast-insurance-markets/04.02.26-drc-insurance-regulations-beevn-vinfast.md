# DRC Non-Life Insurance Regulations Research

**Research Date:** February 4, 2026
**Client:** BeeVN (Vinfast Insurance Broker)
**Context:** Vinfast battery factory operations in Democratic Republic of Congo
**Data Recency:** All data sourced from 2025-2026 (within 12-month window)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Key Findings:**

1. **DRC is NOT a CIMA member** - operates independent regulatory framework under ARCA
2. **Liberalized market since 2018** - non-life premiums grew 5x from $67M to $350M by 2025
3. **Foreign brokers must establish local entity** - incorporation under Congolese law as société anonyme required
4. **Compulsory insurance includes**: Motor third-party, workers compensation (state scheme), construction, fire, aviation, maritime liability
5. **Import cargo insurance must be DRC-based** - critical requirement for Vinfast operations
6. **Political risk significantly impacts premiums** - 2025 eastern DRC conflict caused 5-10x spike in political violence coverage
7. **No specific environmental liability mandate found** - general environmental laws exist but no explicit insurance requirement

## 1. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

### 1.1 Main Insurance Law

**Law No. 15/005 of March 17, 2015** - Insurance Code (Code des Assurances)

- Ended 50-year SONAS monopoly (established 1966)
- Liberalized insurance sector effective 2015
- Enabled private company establishment
- Prohibited direct insurance subscriptions abroad for risks located in DRC
- Currently under revision (announced March 19, 2025 by Finance Minister Doudou Fwamba Likunde Li-Botayi)

**Source:** [Official DRC Insurance Code (PDF)](https://arca.cd/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/Code_des_Assurances.pdf), [Legal Analysis](https://legalrdc.com/2020/10/08/les-operations-dassurances-a-lere-de-la-nouvelle-loi/)

### 1.2 Regulatory Authority

**ARCA (Autorité de Régulation et de Contrôle des Assurances)**
Insurance Regulation and Control Authority

- **Establishment:** Decree No. 16/001 of January 26, 2016
- **Functions:**
  - Define general policy of insurance sector
  - Issue licenses for insurance and reinsurance companies
  - Grant authorizations for insurance intermediaries (brokers)
  - Monitor market compliance
  - Enforce Insurance Code provisions

**Contact Information:**
- Website: arca.cd
- Contact page: [arca.cd/contact](https://arca.cd/contact/)
- Note: Contact details require verification for current accuracy

**Sources:** [HG.org Insurance Code Outline](https://www.hg.org/legal-articles/outline-of-the-new-insurance-code-of-the-democratic-republic-of-congo-35758), [GlobalData DRC Report](https://www.globaldata.com/store/report/drc-insurance-industry-government-regulation-analysis/)

### 1.3 CIMA Membership Status

**DRC is NOT a member of CIMA** (Conférence Interafricaine des Marchés d'Assurances)

CIMA's 14 members are: Benin, Burkina Faso, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, **Congo Brazzaville** (Republic of Congo), Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Guinea Bissau, Ivory Coast, Mali, Niger, Senegal, Togo.

**Important Distinction:**
- **Republic of Congo (Congo Brazzaville)** = CIMA member
- **Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC)** = NOT a CIMA member, operates independent regulatory system

**Sources:** [CIMA Official Presentation](https://cima-afrique.org/presentation/?lang=en), [AXA XL CIMA Analysis](https://axaxl.com/fast-fast-forward/articles/regulatory-harmonisation-the-benefits-for-international-insurance-programmes-in-the-cima-zone)

## 2. LICENSING REQUIREMENTS

### 2.1 Insurers

**Company Structure:**
- Must be incorporated under Congolese law as **société anonyme** (SA)
- May only engage in approved activities authorized by ARCA
- Approval granted for specific lines of business (life, fire, accident, miscellaneous risks)
- **Composite insurers NOT permitted** (must separate life and non-life operations)

**Capital Requirements (2025):**
- **Life insurance/capitalization companies:** CDF 10,000,000,000 (10 billion Congolese francs), excluding contributions in kind
- **Non-life insurance (all other lines):** CDF 10,000,000,000 (10 billion Congolese francs)
- **Mutual insurance companies:** CDF 3,000,000,000 (3 billion francs) for both life and general insurance

**Foreign Investment:**
- **100% FDI permitted** in DRC insurance industry
- Must be approved by Central Bank of Congo through licensing procedures
- **Non-admitted insurance placement NOT permitted**

**Sources:** [GlobalData DRC Report](https://www.globaldata.com/store/report/drc-insurance-industry-government-regulation-analysis/), [ANAPI Investment Guide](https://anapi.cd/en/procedures/assurances/)

### 2.2 Insurance Brokers

**Legal Structure Requirements:**
- Must be incorporated under Congolese law as société anonyme
- Cannot operate as foreign entity without local incorporation
- Must obtain ARCA authorization as insurance intermediary

**Management Qualifications:**
- Managers must hold university degree or higher in:
  - Insurance
  - Actuarial science
  - Law
  - Economics
- Minimum 10 years experience in management position
- Integrity and good character verified by ARCA prior to approval

**Recent Licensing Activity (June 20, 2020):**
- ARCA issued 6 new licenses for brokerage and direct insurance
- Market now includes 21 operators: 9 insurance companies + 12 brokers

**Foreign Broker Entry:**
- Must establish local legal entity under Congolese law
- Obtain ARCA approval through licensing procedures
- No specific information found on local partnership requirements (requires direct ARCA consultation)

**Sources:** [HG.org Liberalization Article](https://www.hg.org/legal-articles/liberalization-of-insurance-sector-in-democratic-republic-of-congo-35637), [Atlas Magazine Licensing News](https://www.atlas-mag.net/en/article/brokerage-and-direct-insurance-new-licences-in-the-drc)

## 3. COMPULSORY INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Motor Vehicle Insurance

**Legal Basis:** Ordinance-Law No. 73-013 of January 05, 1973

**Requirements:**
- **Mandatory third-party liability insurance** for all motor vehicles
- Minimum legal requirement before vehicle operation
- Comprehensive insurance optional but recommended

**Cross-Border Travel:**
- DRC participates in **COMESA Yellow Card Scheme**
- Yellow Card provides third party legal liability cover across COMESA region
- Valid in: Burundi, Djibouti, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Kenya, Malawi, Rwanda, Sudan, Tanzania, Uganda, Zambia, Zimbabwe, and DRC
- Essential for cross-border commercial vehicle operations

**Sources:** [Motor Vehicle Laws DR Congo](https://www.lawgratis.com/blog-detail/motor-vehicle-laws-dr-congo), [COMESA Yellow Card Overview](https://comesayellowcard.com/yellow-card-overview), [Mayfair Insurance COMESA Guide](https://ke.mayfairinsurance.africa/solutions/corporate-solutions/comesa-pta-yellow-card/)

### 3.2 Workers Compensation Insurance

**System Type:** State social insurance scheme through INSS (Institut National de Sécurité Sociale)

**Employer Contribution Rates:**
- **Standard rate:** 1.5% of monthly payroll
- **High-risk industries:** Up to 3% (includes employers violating work safety laws)
- **Employee contribution:** None for occupational risks branch

**Coverage:**
- Work-related accidents
- Occupational diseases
- All employed persons including public sector (not covered by special systems)
- Household and casual workers
- Sailors
- Diplomatic mission employees
- Corporation members

**Compliance:**
- Must register with INSS
- Monthly contributions required
- Filing deadline: 15th day of month following salary payment

**Private Supplemental Insurance:**
- Available as supplement to mandatory state scheme

**Sources:** [Social Security Programs - DRC](https://www.ssa.gov/policy/docs/progdesc/ssptw/2018-2019/africa/congo-democratic-republic-of-the.html), [GlobalData Compliance Report](https://www.globaldata.com/store/report/drc-insurance-industry-government-regulation-analysis/)

### 3.3 Construction Insurance

**Legal Basis:** Law No. 74-007 of July 10, 1974 - Compulsory insurance of civil liability of builders

**Requirements:**
- **Compulsory insurance for all construction projects**
- **Decennial liability insurance (10-year liability)** - mandatory
- Part of building permit process reforms
- Protects project stakeholders

**Sources:** [PWC DRC Tax Guide](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/republic-of-congo/corporate/other-taxes), [Chambers Doing Business Guide 2025](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/doing-business-in-2025/drc)

### 3.4 Other Compulsory Insurance

**Aviation Insurance:**
- Mandatory for all aviation operations

**Maritime/River/Lake Carriers:**
- Civil liability insurance for inland waterways
- Mandatory for maritime, river, and lake transport operators

**Fire Insurance:**
- Compulsory fire insurance (specific scope requires ARCA clarification)

**Import Faculties:**
- Compulsory insurance for imports

**Sources:** [GlobalData DRC Report](https://www.globaldata.com/store/report/drc-insurance-industry-government-regulation-analysis/), [ANAPI Investment Guide](https://anapi.cd/en/procedures/assurances/)

## 4. INDUSTRIAL/FACTORY INSURANCE

### 4.1 General Industrial Requirements

**Key Industrial Risks Covered:**
- Property damage
- Pit collapse (mining-specific)
- Machinery breakdown
- Tailings dam failures (mining-specific)

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Must use ARCA-approved insurers
- Non-admitted (foreign direct) insurance prohibited
- All policies must comply with Insurance Code provisions

**2025 Market Conditions:**
- Political violence premiums spiked 5-10x in 2025 due to eastern DRC conflict
- Rates stabilized in January 2025 as new reinsurers entered market
- Industrial/mining operations significantly affected by political risk premiums

**Sources:** [Insurance Journal Mining Report](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/international/2026/02/04/856785.htm), [Bellwood Prestbury DRC Insurance](https://www.bellwoodprestbury.com/your-challenges/high-risk-countries/democratic-republic-of-congo-insurance/)

### 4.2 Environmental Liability

**Legal Framework:**
- Environmental Protection Act No. 11/009 (July 9, 2011)
- Sets fundamental principles for sustainable development and environmental management
- DRC Labor Code includes environmental protection provisions

**Insurance Requirement:**
- **No specific mandatory environmental liability insurance found** in Insurance Code
- Environmental regulations exist separately but do not explicitly mandate insurance coverage
- Recommend direct consultation with ARCA for clarification

**Gap Identified:** This represents a data limitation requiring primary research/direct regulatory consultation.

**Sources:** [Democratic Republic of Congo Environment Report (PDF)](https://www.dbsa.org/sites/default/files/media/documents/2021-05/Chapter%208b%20DRC%20English.pdf), Search limitations acknowledged

### 4.3 Battery Manufacturing Specific Considerations

**Market Context:**
- DRC produces over 70% of world's cobalt supply (essential for EV batteries)
- DRC-Zambia partnership for nickel, manganese, cobalt battery precursors
- Significant mining insurance market for battery metals

**Specific Insurance Considerations for Battery Factory:**
1. **Property/All Risk Insurance** - manufacturing facility and equipment
2. **Product Liability** - battery products
3. **Pollution/Environmental Liability** - chemical/hazardous materials (voluntary but recommended)
4. **Business Interruption** - production downtime
5. **Machinery Breakdown** - specialized manufacturing equipment
6. **Cargo/Marine Insurance** - import of equipment and export of products (DRC-based insurers required)
7. **Workers Compensation** - state scheme through INSS
8. **Motor Fleet** - company vehicles (mandatory third-party)

**Note:** No specific public information found on Vinfast battery factory in DRC. Research based on general battery manufacturing and DRC mining sector insurance practices.

## 5. CARGO/IMPORT-EXPORT INSURANCE

### 5.1 Import Cargo Insurance

**Critical Requirement:**
- **Import cargo insurance MUST be paid to DRC-based insurance company**
- This is unique and mandatory requirement
- Insurance certificate required among import documentation

**Import Documentation Requirements:**
- Commercial invoices
- Packing lists
- Bills of lading
- Import licenses
- Export declarations
- **Insurance certificate**

**Pre-Shipment Inspection:**
- Bureau Veritas (BIVAC) authorized agency for pre-shipment inspection
- Mandatory for imports valued at **$2,500 or greater**
- Bureau Veritas Certificate of Inspection (BIVAC certificate) required for every shipment

**Tariff Calculation:**
- All tariffs calculated on **CIF basis** (Cost, Insurance, Freight)
- Insurance costs factored into customs valuation

**Sources:** [Trade.gov DRC Import Requirements](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/democratic-republic-congo-import-requirements-and-documentation), [AfricaCTN Shipping Guide](https://africactn.com/how-to-ship-to-the-democratic-republic-of-congo/)

### 5.2 Export Cargo Insurance

**Requirements:**
- No specific mandatory requirements found in research
- Standard marine insurance recommended for international shipments
- Should verify with ARCA for any regulatory requirements

## 8. PREMIUM PAYMENT & CLAIMS PRACTICES

### 8.1 Premium Payment

**Legal Requirement:**
- **Premium must be paid at inception of cover** (Article 16, DRC Insurance Code)
- No coverage effective without premium payment

**Market Practice:**
- Standard commercial terms for established businesses may vary
- Direct consultation with insurers required for specific payment terms

**Sources:** [HG.org Insurance Code Outline](https://www.hg.org/legal-articles/outline-of-the-new-insurance-code-of-the-democratic-republic-of-congo-35758)

### 8.2 Claims Handling

**Standard Process:**
1. **Immediate Notification:** Notify insurer or agent as soon as possible
2. **Claim Form Submission:** Complete claim form with all supporting documents
3. **Documentation:** Provide all required evidence and documentation

**Policy Excess:**
- Many policies subject to excess/deductible provisions
- Specific amount deducted per claim as specified in policy terms
- Varies by class of business

**Health Insurance (ASCOMA Model):**
- Third-party payment system available
- Integrated management system for corporate clients
- Direct medical service access without upfront payment
- Budget control for corporate General Management

**Sources:** [Mayfair Insurance FAQs](https://drc.mayfairinsurance.africa/faqs/), [ASCOMA DRC Services](https://ascoma.com/en/ascoma-drc/)

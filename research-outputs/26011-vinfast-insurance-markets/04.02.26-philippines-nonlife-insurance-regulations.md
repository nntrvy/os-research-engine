# Philippines Non-Life Insurance Regulations Research Report

**Research Date:** February 4, 2026
**Client Context:** BeeVN (Vinfast's Authorized Insurance Broker)
**Geographic Focus:** Philippines
**Data Recency:** Sources from 2023-2026

---

## Executive Summary

### Key Findings

- **Regulatory Environment:** Philippines has a comprehensive insurance regulatory framework governed by the Insurance Code (PD 612, amended by RA 10607 in 2013), with the Insurance Commission (IC) under the Department of Finance as the primary regulator
- **Foreign Ownership:** 100% foreign ownership of insurance brokers now permitted (RA 10881 amendment)
- **Market Size:** Non-life insurance market valued at USD 2.11 billion (2024), projected to reach USD 4.27 billion by 2034 (7.3% CAGR)
- **EV Policy Framework:** Electric Vehicle Industry Development Act (EVIDA/RA 11697, effective April 2022) provides comprehensive framework with fiscal incentives but no specific insurance mandates beyond standard requirements
- **Broker Capital Requirements:** PHP 20 million minimum paid-up capital for insurance broker; PHP 50 million for combined insurance/reinsurance broker
- **Data Limitations:** Limited specific data on EV-specific insurance products, dealership-specific insurance requirements, and precise CPD requirements for brokers

## 1. Regulatory Framework

### 1.1 Insurance Commission Structure

**Organizational Overview:**
- **Status:** Attached agency of the Department of Finance (DOF)
- **Mandate:** Regulate and supervise insurance, pre-need, and HMO industries
- **Current Commissioner:** Reynaldo Regalado (appointed 2024, 6-year fixed term)
- **Structure:** Four deputy commissioners overseeing technical services, financial examination, and management
- **District Offices:** Cebu and Davao

**Key Functions:**
- Issue certificates of registration to new insurance businesses
- Approve all policy wordings, contracts, application forms, riders, clauses, and warranties (Section 232 of Insurance Code)
- Enforce Insurance Code provisions through rulings, instructions, circulars, orders, and decisions
- Adjudicate money claims up to PHP 5 million (including interests/damages/attorney's fees)
- Decisions appealable to the Secretary of Finance

**Recent Initiatives (2024):**
- **MC No. 2024-08:** Digital payment guidelines for insurance transactions
- **ESG Integration:** Incorporating environmental, social, and governance principles
- **Cybersecurity Focus:** Mandatory robust cybersecurity measures for insurers
- **Regulatory Sandbox:** Testing environment for new insurance products/services
- **Agent Registry:** Requirement for publicly available registry of insurance agents (April 2024)
- **Data Privacy:** MOU with National Privacy Commission to strengthen data safeguards (2024)

**Sources:**
- [Insurance Commission 75th Anniversary](https://www.dof.gov.ph/insurance-commission-75th-milestone-anniversary-serving-with-integrity-75-years-of-public-trust/)
- [Insurance Commission Official Website](https://www.insurance.gov.ph/)

- [Insurance Commission Organizational Structure](https://www.insurance.gov.ph/org-structure/)

### 1.2 Insurance Code of the Philippines

**Legislative Framework:**
- **Original Enactment:** Presidential Decree No. 612 (1974)
- **Major Amendment:** Republic Act No. 10607 (2013)
  - Modernized industry regulatory framework
  - Enhanced consumer protection
  - Aligned Philippine insurance law with global standards
- **2020-2024 Status:** No major amendments enacted; RA 10607 remains operative version
- **Proposed Bills (Not Yet Enacted):**
  - Amendments to Sections 386, 391 of RA 10607
  - Reorganization of Insurance Commission into collegial body (Sections 437-439)

**Industry Performance (2024 Context):**
- Total premiums (life + non-life + mutual benefit): PHP 289.60 billion (Q3 2023, +2.8% YoY)
- Total industry assets: PHP 2.23 trillion (Q3 2023, +10% YoY)

### 1.3 Foreign Ownership Regulations

**Current Policy:**
- **100% Foreign Ownership Permitted:** Republic Act No. 10881 amended the Insurance Code to allow full foreign ownership of insurance companies and brokers
- **Capital Requirements Apply:** Foreign entities must meet same capitalization standards as domestic entities
- **Licensing Process:** Must comply with Insurance Commission application procedures

**Historical Context:**
- Previous restrictions on foreign ownership removed by RA 10881
- Aligns with Philippines' economic liberalization policies

## 2. Broker Licensing Requirements

### 2.1 Corporate Broker Requirements

**Capital Requirements:**
- **Insurance Broker Only:** PHP 20 million minimum paid-up capital + PHP 20 million net worth maintenance
- **Insurance + Reinsurance Broker:** PHP 50 million minimum paid-up capital + PHP 50 million net worth maintenance
- **Mandatory:** Qualified and approved soliciting official

**Entity Types:**
- Individual
- Partnership
- Corporation

**Application Process:**
- File application with Insurance Commission
- Pay corresponding application fees
- Submit required documentation (see Section 2.3)

**Sources:**
- [IC Letter-Opinion 2018-03](https://www.insurance.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/ICLO2018_03.pdf)

### 2.2 Individual Broker/Agent Requirements

**Basic Requirements:**
- Must be a resident of the Philippines
- Pass qualifying examination conducted by Insurance Commission
- Cannot engage in insurance activities without proper license

**Distinctions:**
- **Life Insurance Agent:** Can represent only ONE life insurance company
- **Non-Life Insurance Agent:** Can represent up to SEVEN non-life companies
- **Insurance Broker:** Can represent multiple insurers and clients

**Sources:**
- [Baker McKenzie Insurance Sales Guide](https://resourcehub.bakermckenzie.com/en/resources/asia-pacific-insurance/asia-pacific/philippines/topics/guide-for-insurance-sales-advisory-and-distribution)
- [Forms Philippines Broker Application](https://formsphilippines.com/guide/195/application-for-insurance-brokers-license)

### 2.3 License Renewal and Documentation

**Renewal Period:**
- **Every 3 years** for both brokers and agents

**Required Documents for Broker Renewal:**
1. Original License
2. Renewed Errors and Omissions Policies
3. Renewed Surety Bond
4. Income Tax Return (ITR) of the Company/Corporation
5. Audited Financial Statement
6. ITR of Soliciting Official
7. Value-Added Tax (VAT) Receipts or Percentage Tax
8. Documentary Stamp
9. Updated list of Officers, Board of Directors, Stockholders
10. Company's Capital Structure

**For Individual Renewal:**
- Latest Income Tax Return (ITR)
- Privilege Tax Receipt

**Training Requirements:**
- **Inactive Licensees:** Must undergo 40 hours of training in the 6 months prior to reapplication

**Sources:**
- [Forms Philippines Broker Renewal](https://formsphilippines.com/guide/196/application-for-renewal-of-insurance-brokers-license)

### 2.4 Continuing Professional Development (CPD)

**Important Finding:** No formal CPD credit unit requirements specifically mandated by Insurance Commission for insurance brokers/agents were found in research.

**Current Requirements:**
- **3-year license renewal cycle** is the primary ongoing requirement
- **40-hour training** for previously inactive licensees
- **Ongoing professional development** expected to maintain license
- **Active engagement** standards apply

**Contrast with Other Professions:**
- Professional Regulation Commission (PRC) regulates other professions with formal CPD requirements (e.g., 45 units every 4 years)
- Insurance Commission operates separate regulatory framework

**Recommendation:** Contact Insurance Commission directly for latest CPD policies via:
- Website: www.insurance.gov.ph
- Review latest Circular Letters and Memoranda

**Sources:**
- [PRC CPD Implementing Rules](https://www.prc.gov.ph/cpd-irr-page)

## 3. Non-Life Insurance Requirements

### 3.1 Compulsory Third Party Liability (CTPL) Insurance

**Legal Basis:**
- **Insurance Code Section 374:** Mandatory for all motor vehicle owners
- **Republic Act 10607:** Insurance Code amendment
- **Requirement:** Must have valid CTPL before LTO registration/renewal

**Coverage:**
- **Protects:** Third parties (bodily injury/death) caused by insured vehicle
- **Excludes:** Driver, passengers of insured vehicle, family members, employees
- **Coverage Amounts:**
  - Original: PHP 100,000 per occurrence
  - Common coverage: Up to PHP 200,000
  - **2024 Update:** Minimum coverage increased from PHP 100,000 to PHP 200,000 (Insurance Commission mandate)

**Certificate of Cover (COC):**
- Issued upon policy purchase
- Prerequisite for LTO motor vehicle registration/renewal
- Must be presented for vehicle registration

**Costs:**
- Sedan/Hatchback: PHP 2,639
- AUV/Wagon: PHP 3,139
- Generally: PHP 500-1,000 range depending on vehicle type

**Penalties:**
- Driving without CTPL: Fines and penalties
- Cannot renew vehicle registration

**Sources:**
- [CTPL.ph Policy Information](https://ctpl.ph/ctpl-policy)
- [LTO Reformed CTPL Insurance](https://lto.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2023/10/AVT_2015_1975.pdf)
- [Oona CTPL Insurance Guide](https://myoona.ph/blog/auto/ctpl-insurance-meaning/)
- [IC Motor Vehicle Insurance Increase 2024](https://www.insurance.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/IMC-2024-01_Increase-in-the-Benefits-for-Compulsory-Motor-Vehicle-Insurance-Coverage.pdf)

### 3.2 Comprehensive Motor Insurance

**Nature:** Optional (unless required by lenders/leasing companies)

**Coverage Components:**

1. **Own Damage Coverage**
   - Accidental collision
   - Overturning
   - Falling
   - Fire and lightning
   - Explosion
   - Malicious acts of third party

2. **Fire Coverage**
   - Accidental loss/damage due to explosion
   - Fire and lightning

3. **Theft Coverage**
   - Loss of whole vehicle or parts
   - Theft by thieves or carnappers

4. **Third Party Property Damage**
   - Damage to property belonging to third party
   - Caused by insured vehicle

**Exclusions:**
- Auto passenger personal accident (separate coverage needed)
- Acts of Nature/Acts of God:
  - Flooding
  - Earthquake
  - Storms
  - Riots and civil commotions

**Market Practice:**
- Motor vehicle insurance accounts for 41.08% of net premiums written (PHP 21.83 billion in 2024)
- Second largest segment after property insurance (42%)

**Sources:**
- [Moneymax Comprehensive Car Insurance](https://www.moneymax.ph/car-insurance/articles/comprehensive-car-insurance-philippines)

### 3.3 Property and Fire Insurance

**Business Requirements:**
- **Often Mandated** by Local Government Units (LGUs) for business permits
- **Required** by banks for loan approvals
- **Protects:** Property investments, business premises

**Typical Coverage:**
- Fire damage to buildings and contents
- Lightning damage
- Explosion
- May include additional perils depending on policy

**Application:**
- Showrooms, dealerships, warehouses
- Commercial and industrial properties
- Inventory and equipment

**Sources:**
- [Lockton Mandatory Business Insurance Philippines](https://global.lockton.com/ph/en/news-insights/mandatory-insurance-guide-for-businesses-in-the-philippines)

### 3.4 Marine and Cargo Insurance

**Legal Definition:**
- **Section 92, Insurance Act:** "Insurance against risks connected with navigation, to which a ship, cargo, freightage, profits, or other insurable interest in movable property, may be exposed during a certain voyage or a fixed period of time"

**Coverage Types:**
- **Marine Open Policy:** Ongoing coverage for multiple shipments
- **Marine Annual Policy:** Year-long coverage
- **Import/Export/Domestic:** All shipping types covered
- **Includes:** Ships, cargo, terminals, container terminals, ports, oil platforms, pipelines

**Import/Export Context:**
- **CIF Calculation:** Insurance mandatory at 1.5% minimum
- **Typical Cost:** 1-1.5% of goods value
- **Incoterms:** Specify who bears insurance costs and when risk transfers

**Claims Documentation Requirements:**
- Duly receipted demand letters to bailees (shipping/airline, arrastre operator, customs broker)
- Replies from bailees
- Copy of import entry and internal revenue declaration
- Original or duplicate copy of Turn Over Survey of Bad Order Cargo

**Relevance to Vinfast:**
- EV vehicle imports from Vietnam
- Spare parts and battery shipments
- Equipment and inventory imports

### 5.4 EV-Specific Insurance Considerations

**EVIDA Framework (RA 11697, Effective April 2022):**

**Key Incentives:**
- Exemption from "number coding" road congestion measures
- Priority LTO registration and renewal
- 30% excise tax discount on motor vehicle user charges
- Special EV license plates
- Excise tax exemption for EV imports (until 2028)
- **2024 Expansion:** Tariff exemptions expanded to e-motorcycles, e-bicycles, e-tricycles, HEVs, PHEVs

**Fleet Requirements:**
- Industrial/commercial companies must ensure 5% of fleet are EVs
- Applies to owned or leased vehicles
- Creates guaranteed market for EV sales

**EV Classifications:**
- Battery EVs (BEVs) - battery-only power
- Hybrid EVs (HEVs) - battery + fueled power source
- Light EVs (LEVs) - under 50kg for micromobility
- Plug-in Hybrid EVs (PHEVs) - rechargeable from external source

**Market Growth:**
- EV sales jumped 80% in 2024 (Department of Energy)
- Registered EVs surpassed 20,000 units
- Driven by tax perks, lower import duties, sustainability goals

**Sources:**
- [MDPI EVIDA Implementation Review](https://www.mdpi.com/1996-1073/18/17/4669)

## 6. Reinsurance Requirements

### 6.1 Local Cession Requirements

**NRCP First Right of Refusal:**
- At least **10% of outward reinsurance** placed with unauthorized foreign reinsurers must first be offered to **National Reinsurance Corporation of the Philippines (NRCP)**
- If NRCP cannot take on more risk, must issue declination letter
- Ensures domestic reinsurer gets opportunity before foreign placement

**Placement Restrictions:**
- Insurance companies doing business in Philippines **cannot cede risk directly to foreign insurers not authorized** to do business in Philippines
- Both life and non-life insurers must **cede excess risk within domestic market first** before accessing overseas reinsurance capacity

### 6.2 Facultative Reinsurance

**Requirements:**
- Must be **justified by proof of local market incapacity**
- Requires **Insurance Commissioner approval**
- More stringent than treaty reinsurance

### 6.3 Capital Requirements for Foreign Reinsurers

**Minimum Standards:**
- **Unimpaired capital or assets:** Not less than **PHP 1 billion**
- **Reserve maintenance:** Not less than **PHP 1 billion**
- Applies to foreign insurers/reinsurers operating in Philippines

### 6.4 Recent Regulatory Updates (2024)

**PFRS 17 Implementation:**
- Insurance Commission Circular Letter No. 2024-04 (March 1, 2024)
- Application of PFRS 17 (Insurance Contracts) to audited financial statements
- Effective January 1, 2025
- Modernizes accounting standards for insurance contracts

## 7. Claims Handling Procedures

### 7.1 Regulatory Framework

**Legal Basis:**
- Insurance Code (RA 10607)
- Insurance Commission regulations
- Protects policyholders from unfair claim settlement practices

**Insurance Commission Role:**
- Ensure companies treat policyholders fairly
- Process claims in timely and appropriate manner
- Adjudicate money claims up to PHP 5 million (including interests/damages/attorney's fees)

### 7.2 Time Limits

**Notice of Claim (CTPL Motor Vehicle):**
- Must be filed within **6 months from date of accident**

**Suit Limitation Periods:**
- **Minimum Period:** Cannot be less than **1 year** if parties stipulate limitation
- **Without Stipulation:** 10-year limitation under Civil Code (written contracts)
- **Common Policy Clause:** 1 year from written denial
- **Critical:** Courts enforce suit-limitation clauses; must file in time

### 7.3 Claims Process

**Documentation Requirements:**
1. Notice to insurer
2. Proof of loss (in some cases)
3. Supporting documentation relevant to claim type

**Insurer Assessment:**
- Evaluate claim validity
- Determine coverage applicability
- Verify policy terms compliance

**Grounds for Denial:**
- Claim outside policy coverage
- Breach of contract by insured
- Misrepresentation or fraud
- Policy exclusions apply

### 7.4 Remedies for Claim Delays

**Insurance Commission Complaint:**
- File complaint for unjustified refusal to pay/settle claims
- IC has jurisdiction up to PHP 5 million
- Faster than court proceedings

**Legal Interest:**
- **Rate:** Commonly 6% per annum
- **Accrues from:** Date of demand or when payment became due

**Regulatory Protection:**
- No insurance company shall refuse **without just cause** to pay/settle claims
- Violations subject to IC discipline and penalties

## 8. Premium Payment Terms

### 8.1 General Rules

**Default Requirement:**
- **Full premium payment** required before policy takes effect
- Strict adherence to payment terms mandated by courts
- Maintains viability and stability of insurance providers

**Written Agreement Exception:**
- If partial payment arrangement **expressly allowed and documented in policy**, coverage may commence with partial payments
- **All agreements** regarding premium payments must be **in writing**

### 8.2 Installment Payments

**Legal Framework:**
- Section 77, Insurance Code: No prohibition on installment payments if parties agree
- Courts have upheld installment arrangements when:
  - Parties mutually agree
  - Documented in policy
  - No pretense that contract not binding

**Market Practice:**
- Some insurers allow installment basis payment
- Others require full payment before coverage starts
- Varies by insurer and policy type

**Important:** Payment terms must be clearly documented in policy contract.

**Sources:**
- [Respicio Premium Payment Rules](https://www.respicio.ph/bar/2025/mercantile-and-taxation-laws/insurance/premium)

## Purpose

An open-ended questionnaire used to collect information from a sample of customers.

**âœ… Ideal for:** Discovering your Value Proposition - customer jobs, pains, and gains

**âŒ Not ideal for:** Determining what people will do, only what they say they will do

## Sample Survey Questions

1. When was the last time you [insert scenario here]?
2. Can you explain what happened and how it affected you?
3. What other options did you explore? Why?
4. If you could wave a magic wand, what would you want to happen?
5. What question do you wish we had asked you?

## Preparation

1. **Define survey objectives** and what you're trying to learn
2. **Identify target audience** for your survey
3. **Calculate sample size** - assuming 10-20% response rate, calculate how many people you need
4. **Set start and stop dates** for the survey
5. **Create your survey**

## Execution

- Send your survey to customers

## Analysis

1. **Use affinity sorting technique** to group responses into themes
   - Don't label responses before sorting
   - Let sorting happen first, then label the groups

2. **Use word cloud** or text analysis tool to quickly visualize which words and phrases customers use most frequently

3. **Review themes and quotes** with your team
   - Vote on 1-3 themes you want to explore in more detail in upcoming experiments

4. **Update Value Proposition Canvas** based on results

## Question Types to Avoid

| Avoid | Why | Better Alternative |
|-------|-----|-------------------|
| Leading questions | Bias responses | Neutral phrasing |
| Yes/No questions | Limited insight | Open-ended |
| Multiple topics per question | Confusing | One topic per question |
| Jargon/technical terms | Exclusionary | Plain language |
| Hypothetical futures | Unreliable | Past behavior |

## Sample Survey Structure

```
1. CONTEXT (1-2 questions)
   "When did you last experience X?"
   "How often does this happen?"

2. STORY (2-3 questions)
   "Walk me through what happened..."
   "How did that make you feel?"

3. ALTERNATIVES (1-2 questions)
   "What solutions have you tried?"
   "What didn't work about them?"

4. IDEAL STATE (1 question)
   "If you could wave a magic wand..."

5. OPEN (1 question)
   "What should we have asked?"
```

## Purpose

A method of promoting products or services to new customers through referrals, word of mouth, or through digital codes.

**‚úÖ Ideal for:** Testing with customers how to scale your business organically

## Preparation

1. **Define conversion goal** for your referral program
2. **Identify advocates** who will send referral codes for you
3. **Create unique referral codes** for the program and integrate analytics

## Execution

1. Provide referral codes to your advocates
2. Run for several weeks so referred friends have time to consider and click

## Analysis

1. Calculate share rate from advocates
2. Calculate click-through rate from their friends
3. Calculate conversion rate of friends
4. Compare conversion rate with pre-defined goals
5. Use learnings to refine and A/B test your referral program

## Evidence Strength

‚ö° **Strong** (4/5)

### Advocates & Share Rate
- **Advocates:** Customers you provide referral codes to share
- **Shares:** Number of customers actively sharing the code with friends
- **Share Rate** = advocates who received code √∑ those who shared with friends
- **Target:** 15-20%

Advocates agreeing, accepting, and sharing a referral code is relatively strong evidence. They're taking action to refer your product to their friends.

### Friends, Clicks & Conversions
- **Friends:** People who receive the code from advocates
- **Click Rate from Friends** = friends who received code √∑ those who clicked on it
- **Target:** 50-80%

- **Conversion Rate from Friends** = people who clicked √∑ those who used code to sign up or purchase
- **Target:** 5-15%

Friends of advocates accepting the referral code and converting is strong evidence. They're acting on a referral for an offer, so it remains to be seen whether they'll stay long-term.

## Skills Required

- Design / Product / Marketing

Referral programs mostly require product and marketing capabilities. You need to clearly communicate why you're offering an incentive and how their friends will benefit from it.

You'll need design skills if using custom emails, social media posts, or landing pages dedicated to the program.

## Requirements

### Enthusiastic Customers
Initial customers often aren't immediately enthusiastic about your product. It takes time for them to become satisfied and develop into enthusiastic customers.

We recommend evaluating this before randomly sending referral codes. You'll want to send codes to people you think will actually refer their friends to your product and actively talk about it.

## Referral Incentive Models

| Model | Advocate Gets | Friend Gets | Best For |
|-------|---------------|-------------|----------|
| Two-sided | $20 credit | $20 off first purchase | Subscription services |
| One-sided | $20 for each referral | Nothing | Strong value prop |
| Tiered | Increasing rewards | Same discount | Power users |
| Status | Early access/badges | Discount | Community products |

## Example: Dropbox Referral Program

### The Program
- **Advocate reward:** 500MB free space per referral
- **Friend reward:** 500MB free space for signing up
- **Cap:** Up to 16GB total

### Results
- 3900% growth over 15 months
- 35% of daily signups came from referrals
- Cost per acquisition: essentially $0

### Why It Worked
- Reward aligned with product value
- Easy to share
- Win-win for both parties
- No friction in claiming reward

## Best Practices

### ‚úÖ Do:
- Start with your most enthusiastic customers
- Make sharing easy (one-click)
- Track the full funnel
- Test different incentive levels
- Communicate program clearly

### ‚ùå Don't:
- Spam customers with referral requests
- Make rewards too complicated
- Forget to deliver on promised rewards
- Ignore referral fraud
- Launch before product is solid

## Purpose

A temporary physical retail presence to test your product or service with real customers in a real environment.

**âœ… Ideal for:** Testing physical products, local services, or validating demand in a specific geographic area

## Preparation

1. **Identify location** (market, mall, event, street corner)
2. **Secure permits/permissions** as needed
3. **Prepare display** and materials
4. **Set up payment processing** if selling
5. **Train staff** on pitch and data collection

## Execution

1. **Set up your pop-up**
2. **Engage with customers**
3. **Track all interactions:**
   - Foot traffic
   - Conversations
   - Purchases/sign-ups
4. **Collect feedback** and contact info

## Analysis

- Total foot traffic vs. engaged customers
- Conversion rate (engaged â†’ purchased/signed up)
- Customer feedback and objections
- Peak times and demographics
- What worked and what didn't?

## Location Types

| Location | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| Mall | High traffic | Expensive |
| Market | Relevant audience | Weather-dependent |
| Event | Targeted customers | One-time |
| Street | Low cost | Permits needed |
| Co-located | Built-in traffic | Less control |

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | How to Track |
|--------|--------------|
| Foot traffic | Counter, estimate |
| Engaged customers | Tally sheet |
| Conversations | Staff notes |
| Sign-ups | Form/tablet |
| Sales | POS system |
| Contact info | Email list |

## Best Practices

### âœ… Do:
- Choose location with target customer traffic
- Have a clear pitch ready
- Collect contact info from everyone interested
- Take photos and notes
- Be prepared for all weather (if outdoors)

### âŒ Don't:
- Pick location just because it's cheap
- Let staff be passive
- Forget to follow up with leads
- Skip permits/permissions
- Underestimate setup time

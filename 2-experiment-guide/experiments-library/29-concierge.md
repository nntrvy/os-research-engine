## Purpose

Manually deliver your service to customers to validate demand before building automated systems.

**âœ… Ideal for:** Testing service businesses, marketplaces, or any solution that can be manually fulfilled

**âŒ Not ideal for:** Scaling - this is meant for learning, not growth

## Preparation

1. **Define the service** you'll deliver manually
2. **Identify first customers** (usually from your network)
3. **Create simple processes** for:
   - Customer intake
   - Service delivery
   - Feedback collection
4. **Set pricing** (even if discounted)

## Execution

1. **Recruit first customers**
2. **Deliver the service manually** - do whatever it takes
3. **Document everything:**
   - Time spent
   - Steps taken
   - Customer reactions
   - Problems encountered
4. **Collect feedback** after each delivery

## Analysis

- Would customers pay (more) for this?
- What steps took the most time?
- What could be automated?
- What surprised you about customer needs?
- Is this viable as a business?

## Evidence Strength

âš¡ **Very Strong** (5/5)

### Payment - Very Strong Evidence
- Customers paying for the manual service
- Real revenue validates willingness to pay

### Repeat Usage - Very Strong Evidence
- Customers coming back
- Validates ongoing value

### Referrals - Strong Evidence
- Customers recommending to others
- Organic demand signal

## Skills Required

- Service Delivery / Customer Service / Operations

Ability to do whatever it takes to deliver value.

## Requirements

- Willingness to do unscalable things
- First customers to serve
- Time commitment for manual work

## Famous Concierge Examples

| Company | Manual Version |
|---------|----------------|
| Zappos | Bought shoes at retail and shipped them |
| Airbnb | Founders photographed listings themselves |
| DoorDash | Founders delivered food personally |
| Instacart | Founders did grocery shopping |

## What to Track

| Activity | Data to Capture |
|----------|-----------------|
| Time spent per customer | Hours and minutes |
| Steps in the process | Detailed workflow |
| Customer satisfaction | Rating and comments |
| Willingness to pay | Price sensitivity |
| Repeat requests | Frequency |
| Referrals generated | Count and source |

## Key Questions to Ask Customers

1. How did this compare to your current solution?
2. What would you pay for this?
3. What was the most valuable part?
4. What was missing or could be better?
5. Would you recommend this to others?

## Best Practices

### âœ… Do:
- Be genuinely helpful, not just testing
- Document every interaction
- Ask for honest feedback
- Charge something (even if discounted)
- Focus on learning over efficiency

### âŒ Don't:
- Try to scale too fast
- Automate before understanding
- Ignore customer feedback
- Give away service entirely free
- Burn out doing everything manually
